# Final Recommendation

## 1. Which factors appear most strongly associated with monthly sales?

Based on the simple regression analysis, marketing spend and footfall both show a positive association with monthly sales. Marketing spend explains approximately 16.7% of the variation in monthly sales (R² ≈ 0.167), indicating that increased marketing investment is associated with higher sales. Footfall also appears positively related to sales.

## 2. Which variables should leadership focus on?

Leadership should primarily focus on:

* Marketing Spend
* Customer Footfall
* Inventory Availability
* Customer Rating

These variables are expected to influence sales performance and should be monitored regularly.

## 3. Which variables should not be over-interpreted?

The results should not be interpreted as proof that increasing marketing spend alone will always increase sales. The regression shows an association rather than a direct cause-and-effect relationship. Other business factors not included in the model may also affect sales.

## 4. What business action would you recommend?

Increase marketing investments in a controlled manner while monitoring customer footfall and sales performance. Improve inventory availability to reduce lost sales opportunities and continue monitoring customer satisfaction through ratings.

## 5. What risks or limitations should leadership keep in mind?

* Only simple regression models were completed.
* Many business variables can influence monthly sales simultaneously.
* External factors such as seasonality, competition, promotions, and local events were not included.
* The model explains only part of the variation in sales.

## 6. Why does regression show association but not automatically prove causation?

Regression measures the statistical relationship between variables. It does not prove that one variable directly causes changes in another because other hidden or unmeasured factors may influence the results. Business decisions should therefore combine regression evidence with operational knowledge and further analysis.
