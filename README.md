# Retail Store Sales Regression Analysis

## 1. Business Problem Summary

The objective of this project is to identify business factors associated with monthly sales performance across retail stores using regression analysis.

## 2. Dataset Description

The dataset contains monthly store-level business information including marketing spend, footfall, inventory availability, customer rating, discounts, store type, region, and monthly sales.

## 3. Dependent and Independent Variables

**Dependent Variable**

* Monthly Sales

**Independent Variables**

* Marketing Spend
* Footfall
* Average Discount Percentage
* Inventory Availability Percentage
* Customer Rating
* Region (Dummy Variables)

## 4. Regression Approach

Simple linear regression models were created to study the relationship between monthly sales and selected business variables.

## 5. Dummy Variable Approach

Region was converted into dummy variables. One region was treated as the reference category to avoid the dummy variable trap.

## 6. Model Comparison Summary

The simple regression models indicate that marketing spend and footfall are positively associated with monthly sales. A multiple regression model was not completed because a regression analysis tool was unavailable in the working environment.

## 7. Final Model Selected

Marketing Spend Simple Regression Model.

## 8. Business Recommendation

Increase marketing investment while monitoring customer traffic and inventory availability. Continue evaluating additional business variables before making major operational decisions.

## 9. Assumptions and Limitations

* Only simple regression analysis was completed.
* Regression identifies association rather than causation.
* Several external factors affecting sales were not included.
* Multiple regression analysis was not performed because the required regression tool was unavailable.
